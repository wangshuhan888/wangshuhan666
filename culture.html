Cultural Journey

Travel through 3,000 years of Qi State history and feel the deep cultural codes hidden in this land

01 / 04

First stop in the Qi State capital

Museum of Qi State History

This is the most direct gateway to understanding the cultural character of Zibo. As the ancient capital of Qi, Linzi witnessed the most dynamic era of the Spring and Autumn and Warring States periods. From the artefacts on display to the spatial narrative of the entire venue, two keywords are emphasised: openness and power. Qi culture is not a dusty term found only in textbooks; it continues to shape the underlying character of this city today.

02 / 04

The awe of being at an archaeological site

Horse Sacrifice Pit · Eastern Marvel

The Linzi Horse Sacrifice Pit is renowned for its massive scale and stands as physical evidence of the Qi State's formidable power. Confronted by the densely arranged archaeological remains, one feels a strong sense of temporal compression — the hegemons of the Spring and Autumn period, once abstract names on a page, suddenly become concrete, present in the very ground beneath your feet.

03 / 04

From kiln fire to aesthetics

Ceramic & Glass Hometown · Boshan

Zibo's cultural appeal lies not only in its historical sites but also in the living crafts of today. Boshan ceramics, Boshan glass art, and inside-painting craftsmanship transform everyday objects into collectible works of aesthetic beauty. The most captivating thing here is that tradition is not merely exhibited — it is still being made and still being used.

04 / 04

Border memories stretching along the mountain ridge

Great Wall of Qi · China's Oldest Great Wall

Predating the Qin Great Wall, the Great Wall of Qi is the most profound historical line across this land. It is not merely a defensive structure; it is more like an age-spanning boundary crossing mountains and rivers. Standing at a high vantage point and looking back, you can intuitively understand why Zibo's cultural narrative always carries a sense of vastness.

Zibo Through the Millennia

From the capital of the Qi State to a modern cultural tourism hotspot — the character of this city did not emerge overnight

1046 BCE

Jiang Taigong was enfeoffed in Qi and established the capital at Yingqiu. The eight-hundred-year foundation of the Qi State began here.

685 BCE

Duke Huan ascended the throne and appointed Guan Zhong. The national institutions and economic structure rapidly advanced, laying the foundations of hegemony.

Warring States Period

The Jixia Academy flourished; the Hundred Schools of Thought reached their peak in Linzi, where scholarship and politics became closely intertwined.

Sui–Tang to Ming–Qing Dynasties

Boshan ceramics, glass art, and Zhoucun commerce continued to develop. The city took on a composite character combining handicraft industry and market economy.

Modern Era

Zibo gradually established an industrial system; Zibo became one of Shandong's important industrial cities, laying the groundwork for today's urban renewal.

2023 and Beyond

Zibo went viral through its BBQ, but what is truly remembered is its openness, sincerity, and the city's ability to organise services with remarkable speed.

Practical Travel Tips

Plan your route, pace, and accommodation well — the experience will be much smoother

Getting Here

Zibo Station and Zibo North Station connect to the high-speed rail network. The journey from Ji'nan is commuter-short, making Zibo ideal for a weekend trip or a three-day exploration.

Ji'nan → Zibo approx. 30 min

Where to Stay

First-time visitors are advised to stay in Zhangdian district, ideal for connecting dining, transport, and evening activities. If you prefer a quieter stay, guesthouses in the Boshan direction are a good choice.

Zhangdian / Boshan — two options

Best Season

Spring is perfect for a leisurely city stroll; autumn is best for mountain colours and red leaves. If food is your focus, every season is a good time for an unhurried slow trip.

Good all year · Most beautiful in autumn
